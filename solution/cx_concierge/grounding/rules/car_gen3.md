# The car — Gen3

**Topic:** Car / technical regulations · **Scope:** Formula E Gen3, Berlin 2024 E-Prix (Season 10)

Berlin Season 10 was raced with the **Gen3** car — the third generation of Formula E machinery and, at the time, the lightest, fastest and most efficient electric race car the series had built. (The Gen3 **Evo**, with all-wheel drive and a sub-2-second 0–100 km/h, debuted later in Season 11; the Berlin S10 cars are the **original Gen3**, not the Evo.)

The Gen3 produces up to **350 kW (~470 bhp) of maximum power**, with the **race baseline set at 300 kW** and the extra 50 kW unlocked by Attack Mode (and in qualifying). Its defining trait is **regeneration**: the car can recover up to **600 kW** under braking through powertrains on **both axles** — a front motor that is regen-only plus the driving rear motor. So much energy comes back through regen that Gen3 cars run **without conventional rear hydraulic brakes**, and a large share of the energy used across a race is clawed back on the brakes. Top speed is around **322 km/h (200 mph)**.

The minimum weight is **856 kg including the driver** (FIA Technical Regulations Art. 5.1). One nuance worth knowing when reading spec sheets: the dataset's generation-specs table lists Gen3 at 840 kg, which is a chassis-oriented minimum — the **856 kg with driver** figure is the regulatory one. The cars do not carry battery/power/regen telemetry in the Berlin export; that data is GPS and motion only at 20 Hz, so power and energy are inferred rather than directly measured.

All cars are mechanically and electrically near-identical spec machines, with teams differentiating mainly through the **powertrain (manufacturer)**, software, and how their drivers manage energy and Attack Mode. That parity is a big reason Formula E racing is so close.

**Quick facts:** Gen3 (not Gen3 Evo) · 350 kW max / 300 kW race baseline · up to 600 kW regen, front + rear, no rear hydraulic brakes · ~322 km/h top speed · min 856 kg with driver · spec chassis, manufacturer powertrains.
