# Circuit — Berlin Tempelhof

**Topic:** Circuit / track · **Scope:** Berlin 2024 E-Prix (Season 10), Tempelhof Street Circuit

The Berlin E-Prix is held on the **Tempelhof Street Circuit**, laid out on the apron of the former Tempelhof Airport. The Season 10 layout is **2,345 metres long with 15 turns** (numbered 1–15, with sub-points such as 4.1, 15.1 and 15.2). It is a distinctive venue: the surface is the airport's concrete apron, which is unusually abrasive and low-grip compared with normal asphalt, making tyre and energy management especially demanding.

The lap is divided into timing sectors by intermediate points, with **i1 at 680 m and i2 at 1,600 m**, and the start/finish line at the full lap distance of 2,345 m. The dataset's eight timing loops map onto these positions, with Loop 3 anchored at i1 (680 m, after Turn 4), Loop 5 at i2 (1,600 m, after Turn 11) and Loop 8 at the start/finish line.

The **Attack Mode activation zone is on the outside of Turn 2** — a detail worth pinning down because some older sources place it at a Turn 6 hairpin; for Berlin Season 10 it is Turn 2. The **pit lane** entry is on approach to Turn 15, with the exit between Turns 2 and 3, and a pit-lane speed limit of **50 km/h**.

Because the circuit is a flat, multi-line airfield layout, it tends to produce close racing, slipstreaming battles down its long straights, and frequent overtaking — Round 10 alone recorded hundreds of position-change events. The combination of low grip and long full-throttle sections puts a premium on getting energy strategy and Attack Mode timing right.

**Quick facts:** Tempelhof Street Circuit · 2,345 m, 15 turns · sectors i1 = 680 m, i2 = 1,600 m · Attack Mode zone outside Turn 2 · pit entry at Turn 15, exit between Turns 2–3, 50 km/h limit · abrasive concrete apron surface.
