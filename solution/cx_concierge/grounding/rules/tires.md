# Tyres

**Topic:** Tyres · **Scope:** Formula E Gen3, Berlin 2024 E-Prix (Season 10)

Formula E uses a **single, all-weather treaded tyre** supplied by **Hankook** for the Gen3 era (the Hankook iON). Unlike many motorsport series, there is **no dry/wet compound choice and no tyre-strategy game**: every car runs the same tyre in dry or wet conditions, so there is **no compound strategy** to manage and no "undercut on fresh rubber" the way fans of other series might expect.

The grooved, road-relevant tyre is part of Formula E's sustainability story — far fewer tyres are used per car per event than in comparable series, and the rubber is designed to perform across a wide temperature and weather window rather than being optimised for a single condition. The trade-off is lower peak grip than a slick, which, combined with Berlin's abrasive concrete surface, makes tyre and traction management a real factor in lap time and energy use.

Because there is no compound or fresh-tyre variable, on-track strategy at Berlin came down to **energy management and Attack Mode timing** rather than pit-stop tyre choices — and with no mandatory pit stop at the Berlin rounds, the tyres a driver started on were, in normal circumstances, the tyres they finished on.

**Quick facts:** single Hankook all-weather treaded tyre · no dry/wet compound choice, no tyre strategy · same tyre wet or dry · sustainability-focused, lower peak grip than a slick.
