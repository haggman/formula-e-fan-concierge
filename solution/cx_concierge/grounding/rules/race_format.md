# Race format

**Topic:** Race format & length · **Scope:** Formula E Gen3, Berlin 2024 E-Prix (Season 10, Round 10)

A Formula E E-Prix is **lap-limited as its primary measure, with a 75-minute time cap as a secondary backstop** (Sporting Regulations Art. 5.6). This corrects a common misconception that the race is "45 minutes plus a lap" — that was an earlier-era format. In practice a target lap count is set for the event, and the race runs to that lap count unless the time cap intervenes.

The target lap count can **grow during the race** to compensate for laps run slowly behind a safety car or full-course yellow. The number of added laps follows a **deterministic, non-appealable formula**: roughly `floor( (safety-car + full-course-yellow time accrued before the race is 80% complete) / a reference lap time )`, **capped at 7** added laps. Because it is a fixed formula rather than a stewards' judgement call, the extension is predictable once you know how much neutralized time has accumulated.

Berlin 2024 shows this in action across its two rounds. **Round 9** (Saturday, 11 May 2024) was scheduled for 40 laps and ran **46** (6 added). **Round 10** (Sunday, 12 May 2024) was scheduled for 38 laps and ran **41** (3 added). Both Berlin races were won by **car #13**. There were **no mandatory pit stops** at Berlin — the event bulletin did not specify an Attack Charge pit phase — so strategy played out entirely on track through energy and Attack Mode.

A race weekend builds to the E-Prix through practice and a **knockout qualifying** format (drivers split into groups, then advance to head-to-head duels that set the grid). The Berlin grid is **staggered with pole on the right**. Points are awarded to the top finishers, with additional points available for pole position and the fastest lap, feeding both the Drivers' and Teams' world championships.

**Quick facts:** lap-limited + 75-min cap · added-lap formula (SC/FCY before 80%), capped at 7 · R09 = 46 laps (sched 40), R10 = 41 laps (sched 38), both won by #13 · no mandatory pit stop at Berlin · knockout qualifying; staggered grid, pole on right.
