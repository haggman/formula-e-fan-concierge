# Formula E glossary

**Topic:** Glossary / quick definitions · **Scope:** Formula E Gen3, Berlin 2024 E-Prix (Season 10)

**Attack Mode** — A temporary power boost (300 → 350 kW, +50 kW) collected by driving off the racing line through an activation zone (outside Turn 2 at Berlin). Drivers must use a mandated total time, split across two activations. See attack_mode.md.

**Activation zone** — The marked off-line stretch a driver must drive through to arm/collect Attack Mode. At Berlin it is on the outside of Turn 2.

**Scenario (1/2/3)** — The pre-selected split of a driver's Attack Mode budget: 1 = 60 s + 180 s (short-first), 2 = 120 s + 120 s (even), 3 = 180 s + 60 s (long-first). Re-arming a scenario signals strategic intent.

**Regeneration (regen)** — Recovering energy under braking back into the battery; Gen3 can regen up to 600 kW across both axles. Under safety car, regen can exceed consumption.

**percent_consumed** — The dataset's per-lap energy figure: each lap's share of the car's total race energy, normalized so a finisher's laps sum to 100. Mid-race deltas are meaningful; end-of-race values converge.

**Gen3** — The third-generation Formula E car used in Season 10: up to 350 kW, ~322 km/h, regen on both axles, minimum 856 kg with driver. (Gen3 Evo, with AWD, came in S11.)

**Safety Car (SC)** — Field bunched behind a control car at reduced speed; no overtaking.

**Full-Course Yellow (FCY)** — A fixed reduced speed across the whole track; the field is not physically bunched.

**Lap-limited race** — The race runs to a target lap count (primary), with a 75-minute time cap as a backstop. Laps can be added (up to 7) for time lost under SC/FCY before 80% distance.

**Knockout qualifying** — The group-then-duel format that sets the grid.

**Pole position** — First place on the starting grid, earned in qualifying. Berlin's grid is staggered with pole on the right.

**DNF** — "Did Not Finish": a car that retired before the end (mechanical, crash, etc.).

**E-Prix** — The name for a Formula E race event (e.g., the Berlin E-Prix).

**Round / R09, R10** — Berlin 2024 was a double-header: Round 9 (Sat 11 May, 46 laps) and Round 10 (Sun 12 May, 41 laps), both won by car #13.

**Car number / short code** — Each entry has a car number (e.g., #13) and a three-letter driver short code (e.g., DAC) used throughout timing data.
