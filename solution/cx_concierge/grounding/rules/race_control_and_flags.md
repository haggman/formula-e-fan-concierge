# Race control, flags & neutralizations

**Topic:** Race control / flags / safety procedures · **Scope:** Formula E, Berlin 2024 E-Prix (Season 10)

Race control manages the race through messages and flags. In the Berlin dataset these arrive as **race-control messages** that have been sorted into categories; there is **no single FIA-prescribed taxonomy** for them, so categories are derived from the message text. The headline states a fan cares about are the neutralizations — safety car and full-course yellow — and the flags that signal track conditions.

A **Safety Car (SC)** is deployed when there is a hazard (a stopped car, debris, a recovery) that needs the field slowed and bunched. Cars form up behind the safety car at greatly reduced speed and **overtaking is forbidden**. A **Full-Course Yellow (FCY)** imposes a fixed reduced speed across the whole track without physically bunching the field behind a car. Both neutralize racing, and both matter strategically: time spent under SC or FCY **before the race is 80% complete** feeds the lap-extension formula (adding up to 7 laps), and the slow running lets cars **regenerate and bank energy** (see energy.md), often reshaping the closing laps.

Standard motorsport **flags** apply: **yellow** for danger/no overtaking in a zone, **green** for clear/racing resumed, **blue** to tell a slower car to let leaders lap it, **red** to stop the session, and the **chequered flag** at the finish. Track-limits infringements (running beyond the white lines) and other incidents are noted by race control and can draw warnings or **time penalties**, which are applied to a driver's race result.

For a fan asking "what's going on right now?", the useful read is the **race phase** — racing, safety car, full-course yellow, or chequered — together with any recent race-control message. A safety car late in a close race is one of the most consequential things that can happen, because of its combined effect on energy and on the run to the flag.

**Quick facts:** SC = field bunched behind safety car, no overtaking · FCY = fixed reduced speed, field not bunched · SC/FCY before 80% adds laps and lets cars regen energy · flags: yellow/green/blue/red/chequered · track limits and incidents can bring time penalties.
